FEA Analysis of Hydrostatic Lathe Headstock Assembly

Project Overview

This project involved the structural analysis of a lathe headstock assembly equipped with hydrostatic radial bearings. The objective was to evaluate spindle behavior and determine pressure distribution within the hydrostatic bearing pockets under operational loading conditions.

Engineering Challenges

  • Modeling hydrostatic bearing stiffness.
  • Capturing nonlinear spindle movement.
  • Evaluating changes in spindle-headstock clearance.
  • Predicting pressure variation among bearing pockets.
  • Maintaining spindle alignment and stability.

Analyses Performed

  • Modal Analysis
  • Linear Static Analysis
  • Nonlinear Static Analysis

Key Results

  • Spindle displacement characteristics.
  • Variation in bearing play.
  • Pressure distribution across hydrostatic pockets.
  • Stress distribution in structural components.
  • Evaluation of spindle stability.

Software Used

  • HyperMesh
  • HyperView

Deliverables

  • Deflection plots.
  • Pocket pressure evaluation.
  • Change in spindle clearance.
  • Stress contours.
  • Design recommendations.
