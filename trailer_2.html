Multibody Dynamics (MBD) & Nonlinear FEA of Extendable Transport Trailer

HyperMesh | OptiStruct | MotionView | HyperView
Structural CAE | Vehicle Dynamics | Nonlinear Analysis

Developed a high-fidelity virtual validation workflow for an extendable transport trailer subjected to real-world dynamic loading conditions. The study integrated Multibody Dynamics (MBD) and Nonlinear Finite Element Analysis (FEA) to evaluate structural performance under critical transport scenarios such as road bumps, cornering, braking, and acceleration.

The objective was to simulate realistic operational environments and assess stress, deformation, and structural integrity of the trailer chassis and extension mechanism without physical prototyping.

Engineering Objectives

  • Build a realistic MBD model of extendable trailer system
  • Capture dynamic load transfer under real road conditions
  • Perform nonlinear structural validation using FEA
  • Identify critical stress regions and deformation behavior
  • Evaluate design robustness under combined loading scenarios

Load Cases Simulated (Equivalent Road Conditions)

  • 1g Vertical Load: Normal highway driving condition
  • 2g Vertical Load: Road bumps and potholes impact
  • 0.5g Lateral Load: Cornering and rollover stability
  • 0.5g Longitudinal Load: Braking and acceleration effects

These load cases were applied individually and in combination to replicate worst-case operational scenarios.

Methodology

1. Multibody Dynamics (MBD) Simulation

  • Developed full trailer system in MotionView
  • Defined joints, suspension constraints, and mass properties
  • Simulated realistic road excitation inputs
  • Extracted time-history forces and load transfer data

2. Load Transfer to Structural Model

  • Mapped MBD outputs to FEA model
  • Applied forces at critical structural interfaces
  • Ensured realistic load path representation

3. Nonlinear FEA (OptiStruct)

  • Included geometric and material nonlinearities
  • Captured large deformation and contact behavior
  • Performed static and quasi-dynamic structural analysis

Key Results

  • Maximum stress observed at chassis joints and extension interfaces
  • Axle mounting regions identified as critical load transfer zones
  • Cornering loads caused significant torsional deformation
  • Extension mechanism showed localized stress concentration
  • No global structural failure under combined load cases

Engineering Insights

  • Dynamic loads significantly amplify stress compared to static 1g assumptions
  • Cornering is a governing condition for chassis torsional stiffness
  • Suspension stiffness strongly affects load transfer behavior
  • Extension joints are fatigue-critical regions in the design

Design Improvements Suggested

  • Reinforcement of high-stress joints using gussets or local thickening
  • Optimization of cross-member layout for torsional rigidity
  • Improved axle load-sharing under lateral dynamics
  • Fatigue validation for long-term durability assessment

Impact & Value Delivered

  • Reduced reliance on physical prototype testing
  • Improved understanding of real-world dynamic behavior
  • Early detection of critical stress regions
  • Established scalable MBD–FEA workflow for future projects
  • Enhanced design confidence under multi-axial loading
